About Me

01

Profile

Graduate researcher in electrical engineering with a strong background in embedded systems, FPGA design, and high-speed data acquisition. Research and development efforts are focused on real-time signal processing systems that tightly integrate hardware and software to achieve ultra-low latency and high throughput.

02

Projects

Current projects involve developing a high-speed, Wi‑Fi 6–enabled data logger based on Zynq Ultrascale+ and PetaLinux for real-time, edge-level data acquisition. In parallel, I am implementing AI inference directly on FPGA fabric to accelerate signal-processing pipelines. These efforts leverage hands‑on expertise in custom sensor drivers (e.g. AD7768, ISL26104, CS5532) and full-stack design—from low-level SPI and hardware interfaces to Qt-based GUIs—with results published at academic conferences.
